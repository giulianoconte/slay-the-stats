CARD & RELIC STATS
------------------------------------------------------------------------

SlayTheStats reads your run history retroactively. Abandoned runs are skipped.

Stats appear as a tooltip when you hover over cards and relics during a run, in shops, and in the compendium. They're split out by act -- the act in which you acquired your first copy of the card.

- Pick% -- how often you pick a card when it's offered as a fight reward.
- Win% -- how often you win runs that include a given card or relic.
- Runs -- shown as a fraction of runs the card was in your final deck / runs it appeared on a fight reward screen (e.g. 12/30). For relics, just the number of runs present.
- Buys -- for colorless cards and relics: shown as a fraction of runs you purchased the item from a shop / runs it appeared in a shop (e.g. 12/30).

------------------------------------------------------------------------
ENCOUNTER STATS
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Every combat encounter in your run history is parsed and tracked: how often you fought it, how much damage you took, how many turns it took, how many potions you used, and which encounters killed you on losing runs. Three surfaces:

- In-combat tooltip -- hover an enemy (or its health bar) for ~0.75s during combat to see your historical stats for that encounter, scoped to the current run's character.
- Post-fight tooltip -- after winning a fight, a tooltip on the reward screen compares this fight's damage / turns / potions to your historical average, with a significance-colored delta and a percentile rank.
- Stats Bestiary -- a new page in the compendium listing every encounter, grouped by biome / act and category (weak / normal / elite / boss / event). Live monster preview on hover, multiple sort modes (Name, Times Fought, Dmg, Spread, Turns, Death%), and a Raw vs. Significance ranking toggle. Has its own filter pane subset (ascension / version / profile only -- class and upgrade-grouping don't apply to encounters).

Columns shown across these surfaces:

- Fights -- how many times you fought this encounter.
- Dist -- sparkline of damage-taken distribution across fights. See VISUALIZATION below.
- Dmg -- median damage taken.
- Mid 50% -- the range typical fight damage lands in (a quarter of fights below the lower number, half within, a quarter above).
- Spread -- the Mid 50% range as a fraction of the median. Higher means damage is less consistent fight-to-fight.
- Turns -- average turns.
- Pots -- average potions used.
- Deaths -- runs ended by this encounter as a fraction of total fights.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
EVENT STATS
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Hover an event option button in-run to see your historical stats for that specific choice -- how many times you've picked it, your win rate when you did, and a baseline for comparison. Aggregated by character, ascension, act, profile, and version, same as the card and relic stats.

Multi-step events (The Lantern Key, etc.) bucket per terminal option so each branch of the choice tree gets its own stats. Variable-outcome events like Future of Potions split per offered rarity (Common / Uncommon / Rare / Event) -- the tooltip shows raw Picks counts there since the game doesn't record what wasn't offered, so a Pick% denominator wouldn't be meaningful.

Ancient events (Neow, Pael, Vakuu, etc.) are excluded -- their relic-only options are already covered by the relic stats above.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
VISUALIZATION
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Two visualization layers make the numbers readable at a glance.

Significance coloration. Pick%, Win%, encounter Dmg, post-fight deltas, and other comparable stats are colored relative to a baseline -- green/teal for above baseline, orange/red for below. Color intensity scales with both sample size and deviation, so a moderate deviation across many runs reads as significant as a strong deviation across few. A color-blind palette (teal/orange instead of green/red) is available in settings.

Density curves. The Dist column in encounter stats renders each row's fight-by-fight damage distribution as a sparkline -- a smooth distribution curve with a shaded Mid-50% region and a median line. All rows in a table share the same x-axis, so the same horizontal position means the same damage value across rows -- distributions can be read side-by-side to compare consistency between encounters or characters.

COMPENDIUM FILTERS
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Open the in-game compendium (Card Library or Relic Collection) and click the SlayTheStats button in the sidebar to open the filter pane. The pane lets you tune which runs feed your stats:

- Ascension range -- show stats only from runs within an ascension band e.g. A5+.
- Version range -- restrict to specific game versions.
- Profile -- restrict to a single save profile, or aggregate across all profiles.
- Class -- "All", "Match class card" (use the card's owning class for class cards, all-chars for colorless/curse), or filter to a specific character.
- Group card upgrades -- merge upgraded variants (e.g. Strike+ counts toward Strike) or track them separately. Default on.
- Include multiplayer runs -- include co-op runs alongside standard runs. Default off (only standard runs are counted, matching prior behavior).

Active filters are highlighted in green so it's obvious which controls are diverging from your defaults. Three action buttons at the bottom:

- Clear Filters -- remove all filters
- Reset -- restore to your saved defaults
- Save Defaults -- persist the current values as your new defaults. Your defaults are always used when in-run.

NOTES
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Pick% is sourced only from fight rewards. Shop purchases, event cards, and other sources are not counted.

Win% counts all runs in which the card or relic was present in your final build, from any source.

Shop purchases (Buys): For colorless cards and relics, the tooltip shows how often you purchased an item relative to how often it appeared in a shop, colored relative to your overall shop buy rate. Note that appearances are counted regardless of whether you could afford the item.

Colorless Cards, Event Cards, Ancient Relics, Curses, etc are all tracked.

Mod settings (color blind mode, in-run stats toggle, master off switch) can be configured in-game from BaseLib's mod configuration page. Filtering by ascension, version, profile, character, or upgrade grouping is done via the filter pane in the compendium -- see COMPENDIUM FILTERS above.

FAQ
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Q: Why does a card show more "present" runs than "offered" runs (e.g. 5/1)?

A: With "Group card upgrades" off, upgraded and base versions are tracked separately. "Present" counts all runs the card ended up in your deck from any source -- including upgrading the base card at a campfire -- while "offered" only counts fight reward screens. Turn on "Group card upgrades" in the filter pane to merge them. Starter cards and relics (those you begin a run with) will always show 0 for "offered" since they never appear on fight reward screens.


Q: Why is my Win% 100% but the stat is grey?

A: Color intensity reflects both sample size and magnitude of deviation from your baseline. With very few runs, even a perfect rate stays muted -- the color intensifies as evidence builds up.


Q: Why don't I see stats for a card I've used?

A: Open the SlayTheStats filter pane in the compendium and check your filters. 


Q: I'm seeing "no data" for everything.

A: The mod cannot find your run history to populate data. In this case you'll need to find where your game save data is (such as run history). Set "DataDirectory" to the "SlayTheSpire2" folder which the data is stored in. For Windows it is %APPDATA%/SlayTheSpire2. Note that you need to put the full path in there. Please file a bug report so I can fix the defaults!


Q: I lost my save data after installing.

A: It's not lost. By default the game separates modded profiles from normal profiles. If you want to keep your vanilla progress "pure", then create another profile with mods and use an unlock mod to get your progress. There are ways to transfer save data over to a modded profile -- see TRANSFERRING A VANILLA PROFILE TO A MODDED PROFILE below. You can also use the mod UnifiedSavePath.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
TRANSFERRING A VANILLA PROFILE TO A MODDED PROFILE
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Disclaimer - this is how I did it on 2026-04-24. It may no longer work. To see the most up-to-date way to do this, ask the sts2-modding channel on the Slay The Spire discord.

The game stores modded saves in a separate "modded" subfolder, so installing mods makes it look like your vanilla progress is gone.

- BACK UP your vanilla profile folder first. And do NOT later try to copy modded saves back over vanilla -- modded run history can contain data vanilla cannot parse and will corrupt the file. Vanilla -> modded only.
- Launch with mods loaded and switch in-game to a profile slot OTHER than the one you want to overwrite (e.g. if you want to import vanilla profile 1, first switch to modded profile 2 or 3). The game holds the active slot in memory and would otherwise overwrite your copy on the next save.
- Assuming you want to copy profile1: Leaving the game running, copy the contents of profile1/ into modded/profile1 (same profile number). Save roots are below; you can also open the in-game console (` or ~) and type `open saves` to jump there.
  - Windows: %APPDATA%\SlayTheSpire2\steam\<STEAM_ID>\
  - macOS:   ~/Library/Application Support/SlayTheSpire2/steam/<STEAM_ID>/
  - Linux:   ~/.local/share/SlayTheSpire2/steam/<STEAM_ID>/
- Back in-game, switch to the target profile slot. Your vanilla progress should now be loaded under mods.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
UNINSTALLING GUMM
------------------------------------------------------------------------

I was mistaken - you never needed GUMM to run this mod. Sorry! Here's how to uninstall GUMM:
- In your sts2 install folder, next to your executable, there is an override.cfg file that GUMM writes to. Delete it -- otherwise the game won't launch (Godot tries to load GUMM's loader scene and exits).
- Delete the GUMM installation folder.
- Delete the GUMM file from your game's data folder:
  - Windows: %APPDATA%\Slay the Spire 2\
  - Linux: ~/.local/share/Slay the Spire 2/
